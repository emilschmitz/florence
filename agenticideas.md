# Agentic Ideas for Clinical Check-in Agent

- [ ] Provide a summary to the physician in advance of any session, sending it via email.
- [ ] Send a prepared draft of a section of a clinical note based on the history.
- [ ] Send a section regarding check-ins and stuff, based on the provider notes.
- [ ] Based on past notes and notes from the provider and the check-ins, draft a note or a diagnosis or something like that.
- [ ] Send hard-coded messages to the patient when they submit their check-in (for example, a crisis hotline).
- [ ] Recommend, in milder situations, booking an emergency session with the provider.
- [ ] Detect if symptoms could be side effects of medication and notify the provider.
- [ ] Automatically generate reports for insurance or draft claims.

### Evaluation & High-Impact Ideas

**Top 3 High-Impact Ideas**
1. **Clinical Note Drafting:** This is the biggest time-saver. Pre-populating the "Subjective" section of a SOAP note based on the check-in data eliminates one of the most tedious parts of a provider's day ("charting").
2. **Side Effect Detection:** This is a "superpower" feature. Cross-referencing current meds with reported symptoms and flagging potential side effects adds massive clinical value.
3. **Automated Triage (Crisis/Emergency):** Immediate links to hotlines or suggesting an earlier appointment handles the "danger zone" effectively.

**What else could we automate?**
* **Longitudinal Trend Analysis:** Providing trends (e.g., "Sleep quality has decreased by 40% over the last 3 check-ins").
* **Standardized Screening:** Automatically administering scales (PHQ-9/GAD-7) based on mood trends.
* **Medication Adherence Tracking:** Tracking and providing a visual "Heatmap" of adherence to the provider before the session.

**Conclusion:** Prioritizing "Note Drafting" and "Side Effect Detection" offers the strongest immediate value.
